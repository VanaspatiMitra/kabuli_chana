About the Crop:

K-850 is a high-yielding variety of Kabuli chana (chickpea) known for its resilience and productivity. It grows well in well-drained loamy soil with a pH of 6.0-7.5. Typically, K-850 can produce around 800-1,200 kg of chickpeas per acre. It requires about 450-600 mm of water per acre throughout the growing season, with irrigation being essential during dry periods. Profit margins for K-850 are generally favorable.

  • Soil Composition: well-drained loamy to clay loam soils with a pH range of 6.0 to 7.5
  • Water need per Acre: 1.48 to 1.97 feet
  • Potential Dangers: Soil Degradation, Pest Infestation, Waterlogging
  • Fertilizers: Phosphorus, Organic Matter, Potassium, Nitrogen
  • Pesticides: Imidacloprid ,Mancozeb 75 WP, Fluchloralin
  • Profit Per Acre: ₹118,000 to ₹330,000
